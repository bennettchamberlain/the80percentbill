On the Epstein files -- item fifteen on the 80% Bill -- Min has been arguably the most aggressive voice in Congress, using his Oversight Committee seat and SEC prosecution experience to grill Alex Acosta over his role in Epstein's sweetheart plea deal, calling Acosta's testimony "completely non-credible" and writing an op-ed demanding the full release of the files, declaring that "protecting pedophiles is not a position that is tenable in the United States of America" [8][9][10].

Min is that rare breed of freshman who arrived in Washington with both the credentials and the apparent conviction to back the 80% Bill's core reforms. The question, as always, is whether the machinery of incumbency -- the PAC money, the committee assignments, the quiet dinners with donors -- will sand down those edges over time, or whether the former prosecutor will keep prosecuting.
